Foundation

The Three Pillars of Sustainable Development

Sustainable development rests on the balance of three interdependent dimensions. First defined in the Brundtland Report (1987): "development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs."

Environmental Pillar

Responsible management of natural resources, reduction of pollution, protection of biodiversity and ecosystem services. Agriculture depends entirely on healthy soils, clean water and stable climates.

Social Pillar

Fair and inclusive societies with respect for human rights, access to education, healthcare and decent working conditions — including for rural and agricultural communities worldwide.

Economic Pillar

Financial systems that promote long-term prosperity without depleting natural capital. Includes fair trade, rural development, green economy and circular bioeconomy models.

We do not inherit the earth from our ancestors; we borrow it from our children.

— Widely cited in Agenda 2030 documentation · Antoine de Saint-Exupéry (attributed)

Framework

The 5 "Ps" of the 2030 Agenda — in Depth

The United Nations grouped the 17 Goals under five thematic areas that capture the core values and ambitions of the 2030 Agenda. Understanding each "P" helps you link any SDG to a broader narrative during your oral exam.

People

End poverty and hunger in all forms and ensure that all human beings can fulfil their potential in dignity, equality and a healthy environment. Goals: SDG 1, 2, 3, 4, 5. In agriculture: food security, rural health, women farmers' rights, access to education for children of agricultural workers.

Planet

Protect the planet from degradation, including sustainable consumption and production, sustainably managing natural resources and taking urgent action on climate change. Goals: SDG 6, 12, 13, 14, 15. In agriculture: water, soil, biodiversity, carbon farming, waste reduction.

Prosperity

Ensure that all human beings can enjoy prosperous and fulfilling lives and that economic, social and technological progress occurs in harmony with nature. Goals: SDG 7, 8, 9, 10, 11. In agriculture: decent work for farmers, rural innovation, access to energy for smallholders.

Peace

Foster peaceful, just and inclusive societies free from fear and violence. Without peace there is no sustainable development; without development, peace is fragile. Goals: SDG 16. In agriculture: land rights, conflict over water resources, governance of food systems.

Partnership

Implement the Agenda through a revitalised Global Partnership for Sustainable Development: governments, private sector, civil society, academia and individuals must work together. Goals: SDG 17. In agriculture: technology transfer, trade agreements, international research cooperation.

Timeline

The 2030 Agenda was adopted in September 2015 by all 193 UN Member States, replacing the Millennium Development Goals (2000–2015) with a more universal and integrated framework of 17 goals and 169 targets.

Universality

Unlike previous frameworks aimed at developing countries, the 2030 Agenda applies to all nations. Rich countries must also transform their production, consumption and governance patterns radically.

Interconnectedness

The SDGs are indivisible and interlinked. Progress on Goal 2 (Zero Hunger) supports Goals 13, 6, 15 simultaneously. Agricultural choices affect nearly every one of the 17 Goals.

Agricultural Focus

Key SDGs for Agricultural Professionals

A technical analysis of the Goals most directly connected to agri-food careers, with links to EU legislation and exam-ready content.

2
Zero Hunger
End hunger, achieve food security & sustainable agriculture
Core agrarian goal

Ensure sustainable food production systems and resilient agricultural practices that increase productivity while maintaining ecosystems and progressively improving land quality.

Agrarian connection: Precision farming (drones, IoT sensors, satellite maps), agroforestry, polyculture systems, seed banks, reduction of post-harvest losses.

6
Clean Water & Sanitation
Sustainable management of water for all
Critical resource

Agriculture accounts for ~70% of global freshwater withdrawals. Fertiliser and pesticide runoff is the leading cause of water body contamination in agricultural regions.

Agrarian connection: Drip irrigation, fertigation, buffer strips, wetland restoration, nitrate-sensitive zones (EU Nitrates Directive 91/676/EEC).

12
Responsible Consumption & Production
Sustainable consumption and production patterns
Food waste & supply chains

One-third of all food produced is lost or wasted. Target 12.3 aims to halve per-capita global food waste by 2030 at retail and consumer levels.

Agrarian connection: Supply chain management, valorisation of by-products, organic composting, short supply chains (km 0), eco-labelling, LCA (Life Cycle Assessment).

13
Climate Action
Take urgent action to combat climate change
Carbon & adaptation

Agriculture contributes ~10–12% of global GHG emissions while being among the sectors most vulnerable to climate impacts: drought, flooding, and shifting growing seasons.

Agrarian connection: Carbon farming, cover crops, reduced tillage, agroforestry, biogas from organic waste, climate-resilient cultivars, precision livestock management.

15
Life on Land
Protect and restore terrestrial ecosystems
Biodiversity Soil health

Land degradation, deforestation and biodiversity loss threaten the very foundations of agriculture. By 2030: achieve a land degradation–neutral world and halt biodiversity loss.

Agrarian connection: Integrated Pest Management (IPM), pollinator corridors, hedgerow maintenance, soil organic matter restoration, agro-ecological approaches, set-aside areas.

Key Exam Topic — EU Policy

EU Green Deal & Farm to Fork Strategy

The Farm to Fork (F2F) Strategy is the EU's direct response to the 2030 Agenda. It aims to make food systems fair, healthy and environmentally friendly — from farm to fork.

  • –50% reduction in use and risk of chemical pesticides by 2030
  • –20% reduction in fertiliser use, no deterioration in soil fertility
  • 25% of EU agricultural land under organic farming by 2030
  • –50% reduction in antimicrobial sales for farm animals by 2030
  • Minimum 10% landscape features on agricultural land
  • Reduction of food waste by 50% by 2030 (in line with SDG 12.3)

The F2F Strategy is part of the European Green Deal and intersects with the CAP reform, the Biodiversity Strategy 2030 and the European Climate Law.

Agriculture 4.0 · SDG 2 · 9 · 13 · 15

Precision Farming — Technology in the Service of Sustainability

Precision agriculture differs from traditional farming through the surgical optimisation of production inputs using advanced technology — maximising yields while minimising waste, pollution and resource use.

Drones & IoT Sensors
Real-time crop health mapping → agronomic intervention only where necessary → less pesticide use (–50% F2F target)
Smart Irrigation
Solar-powered drip systems → cut water waste → reduce fertiliser runoff into aquifers → protect SDG 6 & 14
No-Till Farming
Conservation agriculture → preserve soil structure → reduce erosion → store more carbon in topsoil (SDG 13, 15)
Renewable Energy on Farms
Wind turbines, solar panels, biogas from livestock waste → circular energy hubs → reduce methane (CH₄) emissions (SDG 7, 13)
Agroforestry & Cover Crops
Trees + crops + livestock integrated → soil fertility, biodiversity corridors, carbon sequestration (SDG 13, 15)
5°PA: Hydroponics & Aeroponics
Soil-free growing → 90% less water → year-round urban production → food security in cities (SDG 2, 11)
The macroeconomic tension: USDA research shows that strictly reducing inputs (as F2F demands) can lower yields during the transition period, raise food costs globally, and disproportionately harm the world's poorest. This trade-off — environmental gain vs. food security — is your most powerful exam argument. See Maturità Map → Economics section

I numeri della crisi / The Numbers Behind the Crisis

160 milioni di bambini nel mondo sono vittime di lavoro minorile (ILO/UNICEF 2021). Il 70% lavora nel settore agricolo — raccolta, allevamento, pesca. La pandemia COVID-19 ha invertito un decennio di progressi, aggiungendo circa 8,4 milioni di bambini al totale.

Confronto prima/dopo

Proposte di soluzione

Dibattito: pro e contro

Esame Orale

Frasi Utili per l'Esposizione Orale 🗣️

Una raccolta di espressioni pronte all'uso per presentare il tuo SDG, il Padlet e collegare i contenuti del programma in modo fluente e professionale durante il colloquio di maturità. Impara queste frasi a memoria — faranno la differenza!

📌 Intro
I chose SDG [number] because I believe it is directly linked to my agricultural studies and to the real challenges facing our planet today.
Ho scelto l'SDG [numero] perché ritengo che sia direttamente collegato ai miei studi agrari e alle sfide reali che il nostro pianeta affronta oggi.
Perfetta come frase d'apertura. Sostituisci [number] con il tuo Goal!
📌 Intro
The goal I am going to present today is SDG [n], which aims to [main objective]. This is particularly relevant in the context of the 2030 Agenda adopted by the United Nations in 2015.
L'obiettivo che presenterò oggi è l'SDG [n], che mira a [obiettivo principale]. Ciò è particolarmente rilevante nel contesto dell'Agenda 2030 adottata dalle Nazioni Unite nel 2015.
Struttura solida per iniziare. Mostra che conosci il contesto storico.
📌 Intro
I find this topic particularly relevant because it connects the environmental, social and economic dimensions of sustainable development, which are the three pillars we studied this year.
Trovo questo argomento particolarmente rilevante perché collega le tre dimensioni dello sviluppo sostenibile: ambientale, sociale ed economica — i tre pilastri che abbiamo studiato quest'anno.
Dimostra che hai collegato il programma di inglese con quello di educazione civica.
📌 Intro
This SDG is closely linked to my professional training as a future agricultural technician, because in our sector we deal with [water / soil / food production / livestock / winemaking] every day.
Questo SDG è strettamente collegato alla mia formazione professionale come futuro perito agrario, perché nel nostro settore ci occupiamo di [acqua / suolo / produzione alimentare / allevamento / vinificazione] ogni giorno.
Personalizza con il tuo indirizzo. Perfetta per la 5°C con winemaking o per la 5°PA con livestock.
📖 Define
According to the United Nations, sustainable development is defined as "development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs" — this is the Brundtland definition from 1987.
Secondo le Nazioni Unite, lo sviluppo sostenibile è definito come "lo sviluppo che soddisfa i bisogni del presente senza compromettere la capacità delle generazioni future di soddisfare i propri" — questa è la definizione Brundtland del 1987.
Citare la definizione Brundtland è quasi sempre apprezzato dalla commissione.
📖 Define
This goal can be defined as [brief definition]. In other words, the main target is to ensure that [what] by 2030.
Questo obiettivo può essere definito come [breve definizione]. In altre parole, il target principale è garantire che [cosa] entro il 2030.
"In other words" è un ottimo connettore che dimostra padronanza linguistica.
📖 Define
The 2030 Agenda consists of 17 Sustainable Development Goals and 169 specific targets. It was adopted in September 2015 by all 193 UN member states and replaced the previous Millennium Development Goals.
L'Agenda 2030 è composta da 17 Obiettivi di Sviluppo Sostenibile e 169 target specifici. È stata adottata nel settembre 2015 da tutti i 193 stati membri dell'ONU e ha sostituito i precedenti Millennium Development Goals.
Numeri precisi = impression professionale. Imparateli a memoria!
📖 Define
The five Ps of the 2030 Agenda are People, Planet, Prosperity, Peace and Partnership. My SDG falls under the "[P]" category because it focuses on [explanation].
Le cinque P dell'Agenda 2030 sono People (Persone), Planet (Pianeta), Prosperity (Prosperità), Peace (Pace) e Partnership (Collaborazione). Il mio SDG rientra nella categoria "[P]" perché si concentra su [spiegazione].
Usare le 5 P dimostra una visione d'insieme dell'Agenda 2030.
🌾 Agri
Agriculture plays a key role in achieving this goal because the farming sector is both one of the main causes and one of the most powerful solutions to [the problem].
L'agricoltura svolge un ruolo chiave nel raggiungimento di questo obiettivo perché il settore agricolo è allo stesso tempo una delle principali cause e una delle soluzioni più potenti al [problema].
Questa struttura "sia causa che soluzione" è molto efficace per SDG 2, 6, 13.
🌾 Agri
From an agricultural perspective, this SDG can be achieved through practices such as crop rotation, cover cropping, drip irrigation, Integrated Pest Management and precision farming techniques.
Dal punto di vista agricolo, questo SDG può essere raggiunto attraverso pratiche come la rotazione delle colture, le colture di copertura, l'irrigazione a goccia, la difesa integrata (IPM) e le tecniche di agricoltura di precisione.
Usa questa frase e personalizzala con le pratiche rilevanti per il TUO SDG.
🌾 Vite & Vino
In winemaking, sustainability is achieved by reducing the use of pesticides in the vineyard, adopting organic certification, and minimising water consumption during the harvesting, crushing, fermentation, clarification, aging and bottling phases.
Nella vinificazione, la sostenibilità si raggiunge riducendo l'uso di pesticidi nel vigneto, adottando la certificazione biologica e minimizzando il consumo d'acqua nelle fasi di raccolta, pigiatura, fermentazione, chiarificazione, invecchiamento e imbottigliamento.
Per la 5°C: collega il ciclo del vino (programma) all'SDG scelto.
🌾 Zootecnia
In livestock husbandry, moving from intensive to organic farming models reduces greenhouse gas emissions, improves animal welfare and decreases the use of antibiotics — directly contributing to SDGs 3, 13 and 15.
Nella zootecnia, passare dall'allevamento intensivo ai modelli di agricoltura biologica riduce le emissioni di gas serra, migliora il benessere animale e diminuisce l'uso di antibiotici — contribuendo direttamente agli SDG 3, 13 e 15.
Per la 5°PA: collega l'allevamento (programma) all'SDG scelto.
🌾 Latte & Formaggio
The dairy sector, including pasteurization, homogenization and cheesemaking, can contribute to food security (SDG 2) while also raising concerns about water use and greenhouse gas emissions from cattle, which links to SDGs 6 and 13.

History & Philosophy
SDG 8 · Decent Work · Child Labour

The 160 million children still in labour (UNICEF-ILO 2021) are the modern echo of exploitation documented by historians and philosophers.

  • History: Child labour in the Industrial Revolution (England, 19th c.) → Factory Acts → compare to Sub-Saharan Africa today (+16.6 million in 4 years)
  • Philosophy – Marx: Alienation of labour, surplus value, commodification of human beings → confirmed by UNICEF-ILO data
  • Philosophy – Kant: Categorical Imperative — treat humanity always as an end, never as a means → direct contrast with child exploitation logic
  • Italian Constitution: Art. 3 (formal & substantive equality), Art. 36 (right to fair remuneration), Art. 37 (protection of child workers)
  • International law: UN Convention on the Rights of the Child (1989), ILO Convention 182 (worst forms of child labour)
"70% of child labourers work in agriculture — harvesting, herding, fishing" (ILO 2021). In your agricultural school, this data hits home directly.
Italian & English Literature
Social Denunciation across Centuries

Literature has always been the mirror of labour exploitation. Two perfect literary counterparts to the Agenda 2030 themes.

  • Italian — Verga, Verismo: Rosso Malpelo (1880) — child trapped in Sicilian sand quarries, inescapable poverty cycle → parallel to 79 million children in hazardous work today
  • English — Dickens: Oliver Twist (1838), Hard Times (1854) — Victorian child exploitation, industrial poverty → same power structure as modern supply chains
  • Connection: Both Verga and Dickens depict the systemic nature of exploitation — not individual evil, but structural inequality → exactly what SDG 8, 10, 16 aim to dismantle
  • Presentation tip: Quote in English from Dickens + quote in Italian from Verga → perfect bilingual showcase for the commission
Start your oral exam with the website → bridge to Verga or Dickens → this demonstrates mastery of interdisciplinary connections from the very first minutes.
Biology & Earth Sciences
SDG 13 · 15 · Climate & Ecosystems

The technical-scientific backbone of Goals 13 and 15, straight from the classroom programme.

  • Climate: Carbon cycle alteration → amplified greenhouse effect → rising global temperatures → altered growing seasons, extreme droughts
  • Ocean: CO₂ dissolves → carbonic acid → ocean acidification → collapse of marine food chains (SDG 14)
  • Soil: Eutrophication from nitrogen fertiliser runoff → algal blooms → dead zones (SDG 6)
  • Biodiversity: Pollinators & telluric biodiversity protected by agroforestry and IPM (SDG 15)
  • Agri-tech: CRISPR/Cas9 for drought-resistant crops; drone-monitored precision farming minimising chemical input
  • Livestock: Converting ruminant methane (CH₄) waste into biogas — farms as circular energy hubs
5°PA students: hydroponics, aeroponics, ruminant physiology & lactation curve all connect here. 5°C students: fermentation biochemistry, winemaking carbon footprint.
Economics · Estimo · Law
Farm to Fork & Green Deal Trade-offs

The most advanced-level connection: macroeconomic analysis of the EU's ecological transition — the USDA scenarios.

  • CAP: Common Agricultural Policy → how EU subsidies shape farm decisions and link to sustainability goals
  • Market failure: Negative externalities (pollution, GHG) not included in food prices → the economic case for F2F
  • USDA Scenario 1 — EU only: EU cuts inputs → competitive disadvantage vs. non-EU producers → revenue loss
  • USDA Scenario 2 — Middle: EU imposes import barriers on non-compliant nations → exports the production contraction globally
  • USDA Scenario 3 — Global: Whole planet adopts F2F → maximum environmental gains, but severe global GDP drop in agriculture
  • The key paradox: Strict EU environmental standards → rising food prices → food insecurity worsens in poorest regions → SDG 2 undermined to achieve SDGs 13 & 15
Exam gold: Presenting this trade-off shows the commission you can read complexity — climate urgency vs. global economic equity. Very few students get this far.

Interactive Activity: "Giving Voice to the Protagonists"

A documentary role-play activity for your Padlet or oral exam preparation. Build an imaginary interview with a young victim of child labour in agriculture, anchoring each answer to real UNICEF-ILO data. Here are the bilingual question starters.

Background: Kofi, 11, cocoa plantation — Sub-Saharan Africa
Based on UNICEF-ILO 2021 data · 160 million children in child labour · 70% in agriculture · 79 million in hazardous work
# 🇬🇧 English Question 🇮🇹 Traduzione Italiana SDG Link & Pedagogical Goal
1 How old are you, and where do you live? Quanti anni hai e dove vivi? Contextualise geography & poverty (SDG 1, 10)
2 What is your typical day? What time do you start working? Com'è la tua giornata tipo? A che ora inizi a lavorare? Explore workload & rights violations (SDG 8)
3 What kind of work do you do? Is it dangerous? Che tipo di lavoro fai? È pericoloso? Identify hazardous work: chemicals, physical injury (SDG 8.7)
4 Why do you work instead of going to school? Perché lavori invece di andare a scuola? School dropout → intergenerational poverty cycle (SDG 4)
5 Do you like your job, or would you prefer to do something else? Ti piace il tuo lavoro o preferiresti fare altro? Psychological impact of early alienation — Marx (SDG 8)
6 Are there any adults who try to help or protect you? Ci sono adulti che cercano di aiutarti? Absence of state protection & institutions (SDG 16)
7 What have you had to sacrifice? Do you ever have fun? Cosa hai dovuto sacrificare? Ti diverti mai? Loss of childhood & play — social isolation (SDG 3, 4)
8 What are your dreams for the future? Quali sono i tuoi sogni per il futuro? Hope & redemption — why global action matters (SDG 1, 4, 8)
